What is globalization?


In a broad sense, the origins of globalization
date back to the Age of Discovery, among the
15th and 17th centuries, when Europeans explored the Americas
and other regions of the world. More precisely,
It is often pointed out that globalization began at the end of the
World War II in 1945 and intensified
with the end of the Cold War at the end of the 20th century.
The process is still ongoing, especially in the context of development
computer networks (Internet) and new technologies
telecommunications, which have brought populations into contact with
and geographically distant markets.


The role of technology in globalization is:


.-Facilitate access to a greater number of goods and services.
.-Accelerate the learning and research process.
.-Rely on new technologies and access to the internet.
.-Allow you to combine cultures from different countries or geographical areas.
Connect businesses around the world in real-time, enabling
reach new markets and customers, take advantage of the
more affordable construction site and supply opportunities in different
Countries.
